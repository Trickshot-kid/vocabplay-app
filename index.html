What are Synonyms?

A synonym is a word that has a similar or the same meaning as another word. Using synonyms makes your writing richer and avoids repetition. They can differ in formality, intensity, or nuance — so choosing the right one matters!
SYNExamples of synonym groups:
Happy → joyful, cheerful, content, pleased, elated
Big → large, huge, enormous, vast, massive
Walk → stroll, wander, march, stride, amble
Smart → intelligent, clever, bright, sharp, astute

What are Antonyms?

An antonym is a word that means the opposite of another word. There are three types: gradable (hot/cold — there are degrees), complementary (alive/dead — no middle ground), and relational (teacher/student — each defines the other).
ANTAntonym pairs:
Hot ↔ Cold (gradable)
Alive ↔ Dead (complementary)
Buy ↔ Sell (relational)
Love ↔ Hate (gradable)
Dark ↔ Light (gradable)

🔤 Use a Thesaurus

When writing, keep a thesaurus handy. Look up your most-used words and find 2–3 alternatives with different intensities.

🃏 Flashcard Drilling

Write a word on one side and its synonyms/antonyms on the other. Review 10 cards daily for best retention.

📝 Story Rewriting

Take a paragraph you've written and replace 3 words with synonyms. Notice how the tone can shift even when meaning stays similar.

🕸️ Word Web Thinking

For every new word you learn, draw a mini web: the word in the middle, synonyms on the left, antonyms on the right.
